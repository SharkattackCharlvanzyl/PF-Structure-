Auction Format 1 of 3
With Reserve Auctions
The seller sets a confidential minimum price. If bidding does not reach it, the seller is not obligated to sell. The most common auction format globally — fair for both buyers and sellers.
🛡️
Seller Protection
Confidential reserve ensures the seller retains the right not to sell below their minimum acceptable price.
🤝
Fair Competition
All registered bidders compete equally. The highest bid above reserve wins unconditionally.
📋
FICA & KYC Required
All bidders must register, verify identity, and accept auction terms before bidding begins.
🔒 How With Reserve Works
✓Register to bid — complete FICA/KYC and accept the auction terms and conditions.
✓Inspect the property — attend the open day and review the auction pack (title deed, conditions, defect disclosure).
✓Bid online or in person — place bids during the auction window. Increments are fixed and displayed.
✓Reserve determines sale — if the highest bid meets or exceeds the reserve, the property sells unconditionally.
✓Immediate binding contract — the fall of the hammer creates a legally binding sale. Deposit payable on the day.
Auction Format 2 of 3
Without Reserve Auctions
No minimum price. The property sells to the highest bidder — no matter what. These generate maximum competitive tension and often represent the best buying opportunities.
⚡
Guaranteed Sale
The property sells on the day, unconditionally. The highest bid wins regardless of amount.
🎯
Best Buyer Opportunity
No reserve means you can acquire property below market value if competition is limited on auction day.
🔥
High Urgency
Buyers know it will sell — creating active bidding and urgency from the very first increment.
⚡ How Without Reserve Works
✓No floor price — the seller commits to selling regardless of the final bid. Pure market price discovery.
✓Same registration process — FICA/KYC required. Review the auction pack before bidding.
✓First bid opens the auction — once the first valid bid is placed, the property is on the market.
✓Highest bid is final — when the auctioneer calls "sold", the sale is unconditional and immediately binding.
✓Deposit & transfer — typically 10% deposit payable on the day. Transfer through appointed conveyancer.

FAQs
Common Questions
Everything you need to know before you bid.
Do I need finance pre-approved before bidding?▾
Yes — auction purchases are unconditional. You must have finance pre-approved or cash available before you bid. The hammer fall is a binding contract. Failure to complete may result in forfeiture of your deposit.
What deposit is payable on auction day?▾
Typically 10% of the purchase price is payable immediately on the fall of the hammer. The exact amount is specified in each property's auction pack — always read this before bidding.
What is in the auction pack?▾
The auction pack contains the title deed, conditions of sale, defect disclosure, municipal rates statement, and any special conditions. Request it from the auction desk before bidding on any property.
Can I bid online from another country?▾
Yes. Our live bidding platform supports bidders from all 185 countries. You must complete FICA/KYC verification and a deposit hold may be required to activate your bidder number internationally.
What is the difference between with and without reserve?▾
In a with reserve auction, the seller sets a confidential minimum — if bids don't reach it, they're not obligated to sell. In a without reserve auction, the property sells unconditionally to the highest bidder, no matter the final amount.
How long does transfer take after winning?▾
Transfer is handled by the appointed conveyancer. Typical timeframes are 4–8 weeks for cash and 6–12 weeks where a bond is involved, depending on the country, municipality, and Deeds Office workload.
