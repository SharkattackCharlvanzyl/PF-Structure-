3. Bidder Security Deposit & Proof of Funds Requirement

To ensure that only serious, financially capable bidders can participate in your auction, PropertyFinder requires every prospective bidder to complete a mandatory security deposit process before any bid can be placed on your listing. As the Seller, you acknowledge and agree to this requirement as a condition of listing on the Platform.

No bid can be placed without completing the security deposit process. The Platform will block any bidder who has not submitted proof of the required deposit and had it verified. This protects you as the Seller from non-serious bids and reduces the risk of buyer default.

3.1 Security Deposit Amount

Before placing a bid on any PropertyFinder auction listing, every bidder is required to deposit USD 10,000 ("Security Deposit") into their own attorney's trust account (or equivalent client trust / escrow account held by a qualified legal professional in their jurisdiction).

The Security Deposit is held by the bidder's own attorney — not by PropertyFinder. PropertyFinder does not hold, receive, or manage these funds directly. The deposit remains in the bidder's attorney trust account throughout the auction process and is subject to the terms below.

3.2 Proof of Deposit — Upload Requirement

Once the Security Deposit has been made, the bidder must:

  • Obtain a Proof of Funds / Trust Account Confirmation letter from their attorney, on the attorney's letterhead, confirming that USD 10,000 is held in trust for the purpose of bidding on the specified PropertyFinder listing.
  • Upload this document through the PropertyFinder webapp on the specific auction listing page before bidding opens.
  • The document must clearly state the Auction Listing Reference Number as displayed on the listing page (e.g. PF-AUC-XXXX).
  • A copy of the uploaded document is automatically forwarded to [email protected] with the listing reference number for verification.
📎 Accepted proof documents include: Attorney trust account confirmation letter, conveyancer's holding letter, regulated escrow confirmation, or equivalent documentation from a qualified legal professional. Bank statements alone are not accepted as proof of the deposit.

3.3 Verification & Bid Activation

PropertyFinder will verify the uploaded proof of deposit document before activating the bidder's ability to place a bid. Verification typically occurs within 24 hours of document upload during business hours. Bidders are advised to complete this process well before the auction opens or closes. PropertyFinder reserves the right to reject documents that are incomplete, unverifiable, or not on the required attorney letterhead.

3.4 Application of Deposit to Purchase Price

If a bidder wins the auction and the sale proceeds to completion:

  • The USD 10,000 Security Deposit is deducted from the final purchase price payable by the winning bidder at the time of transfer/closing.
  • The deduction will be reflected in the sale agreement and settlement statement issued at closing.
  • The winning bidder's attorney releases the held funds as directed by the conveyancer or closing professional managing the transfer.

3.5 Refund of Deposit — Unsuccessful Bidders

If a bidder does not win the auction, or if the auction does not result in an Accepted Bid:

  • The USD 10,000 Security Deposit is fully refundable to the bidder.
  • The refund is processed directly by the bidder's own attorney from the trust account back to the bidder — no action is required from PropertyFinder or the Seller to release these funds.
  • Refund timing is governed by the bidder's attorney's trust account procedures and applicable law in their jurisdiction.
  • PropertyFinder will issue a Release Confirmation to the bidder's attorney within 3 business days of the auction close confirming that no successful bid was concluded, to facilitate the refund.
The refund process does not involve PropertyFinder holding or transferring any money. The bidder's attorney independently holds and releases the funds. This structure protects all parties and minimises regulatory and compliance risk.

3.6 Forfeiture in Case of Buyer Default

If a bidder wins the auction, their bid is accepted, and they subsequently default (fail to complete the purchase), the following applies to the Security Deposit:

  • The Security Deposit held in the bidder's attorney trust account may not be refunded until the default matter is resolved.
  • The Seller may pursue a claim against the defaulting buyer, including a claim against the held deposit funds, as provided under the sale agreement and applicable law.
  • PropertyFinder's 5% Platform Commission on the Accepted Bid (as described in Section 5.2 and the Online Auction Bidding Terms, Section 5) remains due and payable by the defaulting bidder within 48 hours of the default notice, separately from the Security Deposit.

5. Fees, Commission & Invoicing

The following commission structure applies to all auction sales concluded through the PropertyFinder Platform. Commission is charged on the final accepted sale price and is deducted at the time of transfer or closing. No commission is due until a sale is successfully concluded.

5.1 Success Commission on Completed Sales

The total commission payable on a successfully completed auction sale is 10% of the final sale price, structured as follows depending on how the listing was created:

Listing Type PropertyFinder Agent / Auctioneer Total Commission
Agent / Auctioneer Listed 5% 5% 10%
Private / Owner Listed 5% — 5%
Example (Agent-listed): Property sells at USD 500,000 → PropertyFinder receives USD 25,000 (5%) + Agent receives USD 25,000 (5%) = USD 50,000 total commission. Seller receives USD 450,000 net before transfer costs.

Example (Private listing): Property sells at USD 500,000 → PropertyFinder receives USD 25,000 (5%). Seller receives USD 475,000 net before transfer costs.
ℹ️ When listing: You must select your listing type correctly — "Agent / Auctioneer" or "Private Owner" — as this determines the commission structure that applies. Misrepresenting your listing type is a breach of this Agreement.

5.2 Platform Commission on Buyer Default — 5% (Separate)

This is entirely separate from the success commission above. If a bidder's bid is accepted online and that bidder subsequently fails or refuses to complete the transaction, PropertyFinder will charge the defaulting bidder (not the Seller) a Platform Commission equal to 5% of the Accepted Bid amount, payable within 48 hours of the default notice — as set out in the Online Auction Bidding Terms, Section 5.

Where a buyer defaults, PropertyFinder may retain its 5% default commission from the forfeited deposit before remitting the balance to you. Your right to pursue additional legal remedies against the defaulting buyer remains unaffected. No success commission is owed by the Seller on a defaulted sale.

Transfer costs, conveyancing fees, local taxes, stamp duty, transfer duty, capital gains tax, VAT, notary fees, and all other regulatory costs are the sole responsibility of the Seller and are entirely separate from PropertyFinder's fees and commission. These vary by country and asset type.
