📋 Partner Agency Obligations

To maintain a trusted marketplace for buyers, all partner agencies agree to these standards.

✅ Accurate Listings

All listing information must be accurate and up to date. Prices, availability, photos, and descriptions must reflect the actual property. Misleading or false listings will be removed.

✅ Licensed & Registered

Agency must hold a valid real estate licence or registration in their operating country. We verify credentials during onboarding and may re-verify annually.

✅ Respond Within 48 Hours

Buyer enquiries received through PropertyFinder must be responded to within 48 hours. Persistent non-response may result in listing suspension.

✅ Remove Sold Properties

Sold, let, or withdrawn properties must be removed or marked as such within 7 days. Auto-sync agencies: ensure your CRM status updates are current.

✅ Original Photos Only

All photos must be original and owned by the agency or taken with the owner's permission. Stock photos, watermarked images, and AI-generated images are not permitted.

✅ Fair Dealing

Agencies must comply with local consumer protection and fair trading laws. No bait-and-switch pricing, undisclosed fees, or pressure selling tactics.

✅ Data Privacy

Buyer contact information received through PropertyFinder must be handled in accordance with GDPR, POPIA, and applicable local privacy laws. No unsolicited bulk marketing.

✅ Payment Upfront

All listing fees are prepaid upfront before listings go live. No credit is extended. Choose a billing period (1, 3, 6, or 12 months) and pay in full. No listings are published until payment clears.

✅ No Duplicate Listings

Each property may only be listed once per agency. Duplicate listings inflate search results and degrade the buyer experience. Duplicates will be merged or removed.

✅ Maintain Feed Connection

Auto-sync agencies must maintain a functioning feed connection. If your feed is down for more than 7 days, listings may be paused until reconnected.

✅ Professional Conduct

All communication with buyers must be professional. Agencies reported for harassment, discrimination, or unprofessional conduct will be reviewed and may be terminated.

✅ Brand Guidelines

When referencing PropertyFinder in your marketing, use our official logo and naming. Do not imply PropertyFinder endorses, values, or guarantees specific properties.

⚠️ Breach of obligations may result in a warning (first offence), listing suspension (second offence), or partnership termination (persistent breach). We work with agencies to resolve issues before escalating.

PropertyFinder Agency Partnership Agreement

This Agreement is entered into between PropertyFinder International (Pty) Ltd ("PropertyFinder") and the Agency identified in the partnership application ("the Agency").

1. Services

PropertyFinder provides an international property listing platform. The Agency's listings will be displayed to buyers across 185 countries. PropertyFinder is a marketplace platform and is not a party to any property transaction between the Agency and buyers.

2. Fees & Payment

All fees are prepaid upfront. No credit is extended. Fees are charged per active listing: Private (1–10 listings): $7.50/listing/month. Agency (11–2,000 listings): $3.00/listing/month. Enterprise (2,000+ listings): $2.00/listing/month. The Agency selects a billing period (1, 3, 6, or 12 months) and pays in full before listings go live. No listings are published until payment clears. If listings increase mid-cycle, the Agency pays the pro-rata difference upfront. If listings decrease, the credit rolls forward to the next period. PropertyFinder charges zero commission on any transaction.

2.1 Automatic Monthly Invoice

PropertyFinder issues an automatic invoice on the 2nd of every month for the previous month's actual listing usage. The invoice is generated from the customer details provided when signing this agreement. It serves as a tax invoice/receipt, a reconciliation of actual vs. prepaid usage, a record of mid-cycle additions and pro-rata charges, and a statement of remaining prepaid balance. If actual usage exceeds the prepaid balance, the difference is due immediately. If usage is below the prepaid balance, the surplus rolls forward as credit.

3. Agency Obligations

The Agency agrees to: maintain accurate and current listing information; hold valid licensing/registration; respond to buyer enquiries within 48 hours; remove sold/let properties within 7 days; use original photos only; comply with applicable laws; handle buyer data in accordance with privacy laws; prepay all listing fees upfront; avoid duplicate listings; maintain feed connections; and conduct business professionally.

4. Data & Privacy

The Agency retains ownership of all listing data. PropertyFinder has a licence to display content on the platform and syndication partners. Buyer enquiry data is shared exclusively with the Agency. PropertyFinder complies with GDPR, POPIA, and applicable privacy regulations.

5. Term & Termination

The agreement runs for the prepaid billing period selected by the Agency (1, 3, 6, or 12 months). Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice. Upon termination, listings remain active until the end of the prepaid period. Unused prepaid balance is non-refundable but may be applied to future listings within 12 months. No exit fees or penalties apply.

6. Liability

PropertyFinder is not liable for the accuracy of listings, agent conduct, or transaction outcomes. The Agency indemnifies PropertyFinder against claims arising from listing content, agent conduct, or breach of applicable laws. PropertyFinder's maximum liability is limited to fees paid in the previous 12 months.

7. Breach & Remedies

First offence: written warning. Second offence: listing suspension (up to 30 days). Persistent breach: partnership termination. PropertyFinder will work with the Agency to resolve issues before escalating. The Agency may appeal any action by writing to [email protected].

8. Dispute Resolution

All disputes are resolved directly with PropertyFinder head office. Contact [email protected]. PropertyFinder will investigate and provide a resolution within 30 days. Both parties commit to good-faith negotiation. If unresolved within 30 days, the matter may be escalated to independent mediation.
