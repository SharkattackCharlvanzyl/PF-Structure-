Parties, Purpose & Scope

This Private Seller Listing Agreement ("Agreement") is between PropertyFinder Head Office ("PropertyFinder", "Platform", "we", "us") and the individual property owner or legal entity identified below ("Private Seller", "Owner", "you").

This Agreement applies specifically to sellers who are listing their own property without a registered estate agent, auctioneer, or mandate holder acting on their behalf. If you are an estate agent or auctioneer, please use the Seller / Agent Listing Agreement instead.

By signing this Agreement, you appoint PropertyFinder to host and market your property listing on the Platform and (if selected) to provide online auction functionality, subject to the terms below and all applicable Platform policies.

ℹ️ This is a private listing. As a private seller, you take on the responsibilities that an estate agent would ordinarily perform — including viewings, communications with buyers, negotiation, and compliance with disclosure obligations in your jurisdiction. PropertyFinder provides the platform and technology only.
2

Ownership, Authority & Eligibility

2.1 Proof of Ownership

You warrant that you are the registered owner of the property being listed, or that you have the legal authority to list and sell it (e.g. executor of an estate, trustee, or power of attorney holder). You must be able to provide documentary proof upon request, including but not limited to:

  • Title deed or equivalent ownership document
  • FICA / KYC identity documents (ID or passport)
  • Rates clearance certificate or equivalent (where applicable)
  • If selling on behalf of an estate or trust: grant of probate, letters of administration, or trust deed

2.2 No Outstanding Conflicts

You confirm that no other agent, auctioneer, or mandate holder has an existing exclusive mandate over this property that would prevent you from listing privately. You indemnify PropertyFinder against any claims arising from mandate disputes.

2.3 Eligibility

You must be 18 years or older, or a duly authorised representative of a legal entity. You must not be listed on any sanctions, fraud, or money-laundering watchlists applicable in your jurisdiction.

3

Listing Content & Accuracy

3.1 Accuracy Obligation

All listing content you provide — including photographs, descriptions, stated dimensions, zoning, permitted use, legal title, and price — must be accurate, complete, and not misleading. You are solely responsible for the accuracy of your listing. PropertyFinder does not verify listing content.

⚠️ Misrepresentation is a serious legal risk. As a private seller, you do not have the protection of a professional intermediary. Any misrepresentation of the property — whether in the listing description, photographs, or documents shared with buyers — may expose you to civil claims, regulatory penalties, or criminal liability depending on your jurisdiction. Always get legal advice if you are unsure.

3.2 Photographs & Media

You must own the rights to all photographs, videos, and media uploaded to your listing or have the explicit permission of the copyright holder to use them. You grant PropertyFinder a worldwide, royalty-free licence to display and reproduce this content on the Platform and in marketing channels for the duration of your listing, and for a reasonable period thereafter for record-keeping and dispute resolution purposes.

3.3 Property Defects & Disclosure

You must disclose all known material defects, latent defects, encumbrances, servitudes, bonds, or conditions affecting the property. Failure to disclose known defects may result in the sale being set aside or a damages claim against you. Many jurisdictions impose statutory disclosure obligations on private sellers — you are responsible for complying with these.

3.4 Content Moderation

PropertyFinder reserves the right to remove, edit, or reject any listing content that violates our policies, applicable law, or advertising standards. Listings may not include external contact details (phone numbers, email addresses, or social media links) in the description or images.

4

Your Responsibilities as a Private Seller

As a private seller, you assume full responsibility for all activities that would ordinarily be managed by a professional estate agent or auctioneer. These include but are not limited to:

4.1 Viewings & Access

You are responsible for scheduling, managing, and attending all property viewings. PropertyFinder does not arrange viewings on your behalf. You must ensure buyers gain lawful access to the property and that viewings are conducted safely and in compliance with any occupant or tenancy obligations.

4.2 Buyer Communication & Negotiation

All communications with prospective buyers, offers received, counter-offers, and negotiations are your sole responsibility. PropertyFinder provides a platform messaging system for initial enquiries, but any correspondence beyond that is between you and the buyer directly.

⚠️ Never share banking details, OTPs, or personal financial information via unsecured channels. PropertyFinder will never request this information from you or a buyer via chat or email.

4.3 Offer & Sale Agreement

You are responsible for reviewing, accepting, or declining any offer to purchase. PropertyFinder does not draft, review, or validate sale agreements. You are strongly advised to use a qualified conveyancer or property attorney to prepare and review the sale agreement before signing.

4.4 FICA & Anti-Money Laundering Compliance

Private property transactions are subject to FICA (or equivalent AML legislation in your jurisdiction). You are responsible for ensuring that any buyer is properly identified and that the transaction complies with applicable anti-money laundering regulations. Your appointed conveyancer will typically assist with this, but the obligation also rests with you as the seller.

4.5 Compliance Certificates

Depending on your jurisdiction, you may be required to obtain compliance certificates before transfer — such as electrical, plumbing, gas, beetle, electric fence, or energy performance certificates. You are solely responsible for obtaining and providing these. PropertyFinder is not liable for any delays in transfer caused by outstanding certificates.

4.6 Transfer & Conveyancing

You must appoint a registered conveyancer or attorney to manage the legal transfer of the property. Transfer costs, conveyancing fees, rates clearances, bond cancellation fees, taxes (including capital gains tax, transfer duty, and VAT where applicable), and all other regulatory costs are entirely your responsibility and are separate from PropertyFinder's commission.

5

Commission, Fees & Payment

5.1 Private Seller Commission Structure

As a private seller (no agent or auctioneer involved), the following commission structure applies on a successfully completed sale:

Listing Type PropertyFinder Agent / Auctioneer Total Commission
Private / Owner Listed ✓ This Agreement 5% None — $0 5%
Agent / Auctioneer Listed (for reference) 5% 5% 10%
Example: Your property sells for USD 400,000
PropertyFinder commission (5%) = USD 20,000
Agent commission = USD 0 (private listing)
You receive: USD 380,000 net — before transfer costs, taxes, and conveyancing fees.

5.2 When Commission is Charged

Commission is only charged on a successfully completed sale. It is deducted from the seller's proceeds at the time of transfer or closing. No commission is due if the property does not sell, if the auction receives no accepted bid, or if the sale falls through before transfer.

A tax invoice will be issued automatically at the time of deduction.

5.3 Listing Fees

Separate listing and promotional fees may apply depending on the listing tier and features selected (e.g. featured placement, premium photography, auction hosting). These fees are displayed at checkout and are prepaid upfront. No listing is published until payment has cleared. Listing fees are non-refundable once the listing is live.

5.4 Auction Listings — Additional Terms

If you choose to list your property as an online auction, the Online Auction Seller Agreement also applies in full and must be signed separately. The 5% private seller commission applies equally to auction sales.

On a private auction listing, if the winning bidder defaults after an Accepted Bid, PropertyFinder charges the defaulting bidder (not you) a separate 5% Platform Commission on the accepted bid amount, payable within 48 hours — as set out in the Auction Bidding Terms, Section 5. No success commission is owed by you as the seller on a defaulted sale.

⚠️ Transfer costs are separate from commission. Transfer duty, stamp duty, conveyancing fees, FICA compliance costs, compliance certificates, bond cancellation fees, capital gains tax, VAT, and all other regulatory or closing costs are your responsibility and vary by country and property type. PropertyFinder is not liable for these costs.

See the Commission Breakdown Calculator for an interactive example of net proceeds at any sale price.

6

Listing Duration, Withdrawal & Cancellation

6.1 Listing Duration

Your listing will remain active for the duration selected at checkout (typically 30, 60, or 90 days), or until the property is sold, withdrawn, or suspended. Listings may be renewed at the applicable rate.

6.2 Withdrawal Before Sale

You may withdraw your listing at any time before an accepted offer or accepted auction bid. Prepaid listing fees are non-refundable. Withdrawal does not attract a commission charge.

6.3 Withdrawal After Accepted Offer / Bid

If you withdraw or refuse to complete a sale after an offer has been formally accepted (or after an auction bid has been accepted), you may be liable for a withdrawal fee as set out in the Commission & Fees Policy, and may be subject to legal action by the buyer. Your listing may be suspended pending resolution.

🚫 You may not withdraw an active auction listing once bids have been placed, except with PropertyFinder's prior written approval. Unauthorised withdrawal after bids are placed may result in immediate account suspension and fee recovery action.
7

Anti-Fraud, Shill Bidding & Off-Platform Dealing

7.1 Prohibited Conduct

The following are strictly prohibited and may result in immediate listing removal, account suspension, and referral to law enforcement:

  • Creating fake enquiries or bids to inflate perceived demand (shill bidding)
  • Instructing family members, associates, or agents to bid on your listing to drive up the price
  • Misrepresenting ownership, title, or the existence of a mandate
  • Listing a property you do not own or do not have authority to sell
  • Soliciting buyers to transact off-platform to avoid commission
  • Providing false identity or financial information during FICA/KYC verification

7.2 Off-Platform Transactions

If a buyer introduced to you through PropertyFinder (via enquiry, auction, or listing interaction) subsequently purchases your property outside the Platform — whether to avoid commission or otherwise — PropertyFinder's 5% commission remains due and payable. PropertyFinder may enforce this obligation through legal action.

This obligation survives the withdrawal or expiry of your listing for a period of 12 months from the date the buyer was first introduced via the Platform.

ℹ️ All on-platform communications and bid/enquiry records are stored and may be used as evidence in the event of a commission dispute or legal proceeding.

Data Protection & Privacy

PropertyFinder processes your personal data as described in the Privacy Policy. By signing this Agreement, you consent to PropertyFinder using your contact details, listing data, and transaction records for the purpose of providing and improving the Platform services, and for regulatory compliance and dispute resolution.

Enquiries and buyer data passed to you through the Platform must be handled in compliance with applicable data protection legislation in your jurisdiction. You may not use buyer contact information for any purpose other than completing the property transaction.

Platform Liability & Indemnity

9.1 Platform Role

PropertyFinder operates as a marketplace platform only. We are not a party to the sale agreement between you and any buyer. We do not provide legal, financial, tax, or conveyancing advice. We do not verify the accuracy of listing content, the identity of buyers, or the financial capacity of bidders.

9.2 Limitation of Liability

To the maximum extent permitted by law, PropertyFinder's total liability to you in connection with any listing or transaction is limited to the listing fees paid by you in the 12 months preceding the relevant claim. PropertyFinder is not liable for: loss of sale, buyer default, market price movements, failed transfers, or any consequential, indirect, or special losses.

9.3 Your Indemnity to PropertyFinder

You agree to indemnify and hold PropertyFinder harmless from all claims, losses, fines, penalties, and legal costs arising from: (a) inaccurate or misleading listing content; (b) listing a property without lawful authority; (c) intellectual property infringement in your uploaded content; (d) your failure to complete a confirmed sale; (e) mandate disputes with estate agents; or (f) any breach of applicable law or this Agreement.
